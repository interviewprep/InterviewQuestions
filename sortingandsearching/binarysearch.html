Binary Search

 

The power of binary search is to divide the search space repeatedly into two halves, and just pick one of them for further searching.

So binary searches are a natural fit to search a sorted space; look at the middle element and decide which half to search.

Unlike a sorted search space, the ability to half the searchspace is sometimes not obvious, but binary searches are a good fit as long as the search space can be repeatedly halved. For example, to find out the square root of a number, the search space is from 1 to the number itself. 

Time complexity

The time complexity of a typical binary search is log n because you repeatedly half a search space of size n.
